PART II – INCOME TAX
Section 2 · Income Tax Act
Digital Platforms Now Taxed as "Royalties"
The definition of "royalty" is expanded to include fees paid for using any digital marketplace, digital platform, or payment processing system — whether called a service fee, network fee, or processing fee. Software licences, training fees, and maintenance fees are also included.
→
⚠️ WEIRD!
Netflix, Spotify, M-Pesa merchant fees, card payment fees — all now "royalties". Businesses using card payments will pay MORE. Cost passes to you.
Section 4 · New Section 6B · Income Tax Act
Non-Resident Rental Income Tax – New Category
A brand new tax called "non-resident rental income tax" is created. Any foreigner earning rent from property in Kenya must now pay tax at the rate in the Third Schedule — as a final tax. They must register and file monthly returns.
→
NEW!
Foreign landlords in Kenya now have their own tax lane. Could affect rent prices if foreign-owned apartments pass the cost to tenants.
Section 7 · Income Tax Act s.10
Scrap Metal Sales & Gambling Winnings Now Withheld
Sale of scrap metal (1.5% withholding) and gambling/lottery winnings (20% withholding) are added to the list of payments subject to withholding tax. KRA takes its cut before you receive the money.
→
TAX!
Jua kali scrap dealers: 1.5% deducted at source. Gamblers: win Ksh 10,000, KRA takes Ksh 2,000 immediately. No escaping it.
Section 10 · Income Tax Act s.15(af)
Mortgage Interest Relief — CBK Loans Only
Employees can now deduct up to Ksh 360,000/year in mortgage interest from taxable income — but only for loans advanced by the Central Bank of Kenya for construction, purchase or improvement of their own home.
→
GOOD!
Slight relief for homeowners with CBK loans. But most Kenyans use commercial banks — so this won't help many people.
Section 23 · Eighth Schedule · Income Tax Act
Capital Gains — Non-Residents Selling Kenyan Shares
Foreigners who sell shares that derive value from immovable property in Kenya — or that result in a change of ownership of a Kenyan company — will now pay Capital Gains Tax (CGT) on those transactions.
→
CGT!
Foreign investors: selling your stake in a Kenyan real estate company? KRA wants a share. May reduce foreign investment appetite.
🚬 PART III – EXCISE DUTY
Part III · Excise Duty Act
Tobacco & Alcohol Excise Rates Revised
• Cigars & cigarillos: New rate of Ksh 18,000 per kg
• Other manufactured tobacco: Rate rises to Ksh 12,550 per kg
• Spirits above 90% alcohol strength: definition narrowed
• Beer/cider under 6%: proviso on rates removed (effective rate increase)
→
PRICIER!
Cigarettes and alcohol going up. Government's way of taxing "sin goods" — and raising revenue at the same time. Unajua unachagua tu.
🛒 PART IV – VAT (ZERO-RATED GOODS)
Part IV · VAT Act · Second Schedule
New VAT Zero-Rated Items (No Tax)
New items added to the zero-rated (0% VAT) list:
  • Scrap metal (para. 159)
  • Dialyzers — kidney dialysis equipment (para. 158)
  • Animal feed raw materials imported/local (para. 160)
  • Pharmaceutical raw materials (para. 161)
  • Sugarcane transportation farm to factory (para. 162)
  • Mobile phones — imported & locally purchased (para. 163)
→
✅ GOOD!
Phones cheaper! Medicines cheaper! Animal feeds cheaper (affects unga prices). Dialysis patients get some relief. These are actual wins for wananchi.
⚖️ PART V – TAX PROCEDURES
Section 38 · New s.6C · Tax Procedures Act
Crypto Exchanges Must Report Your Transactions to KRA
Every virtual asset service provider (crypto exchange, trading platform) must now file an information return with KRA for every Kenyan user on their platform — every calendar year. Making a false statement is a criminal offence.
→
₿ CRYPTO!
Your Binance, Paxful, Coinbase activity? KRA will know. Not just taxing — they want full visibility. Anonymous crypto days in Kenya = OVER.
Section 55 · Miscellaneous Fees & Levies Act
Import Declaration Fee — Phones Now Exempt
Imported cellular phones and wireless network phones are added to the list of goods exempt from the Import Declaration Fee (IDF) and other levies under Part III of the Miscellaneous Fees and Levies Act.
→
📱 YES!
Combined with the VAT zero-rating — imported phones will be noticeably cheaper. Great news if you're eyeing a new handset (ahem).
Part V · Tax Procedures
KRA Can Auto-Generate Your Tax Return
If you fail to file your return on time, the Commissioner can issue a pre-populated return generated from data they already have — from banks, M-Pesa records, employers, and other sources. Any amount withheld during an objection or appeal is non-refundable until the case is fully resolved.
→
BAD!
KRA creates your return without you. If it's wrong — you fight it, but they KEEP your money while you fight. You lose cash flow during the appeal. Very unfair.
Part V · Tax Procedures Act s.51
Saturdays, Sundays & Public Holidays Count as Filing Days
The Tax Procedures Act is amended so that when counting days for deadlines, Saturdays, Sundays and public holidays are now included. Previously only working days counted. Miss a Saturday deadline? Penalty applies.
→
TRICKY!
Your deadline window just got smaller. A 7-day notice used to give you 5 working days. Now it's literally 7 days — including weekends. Easy to miss. Penalties = money.
Section 55 · Miscellaneous Fees · Second Schedule Part A
Aircraft Parts — IDF Exemption Broadened to All Aircraft
Previously only certain aircraft spares were exempt from the Import Declaration Fee. The bill removes "other aircraft spare" and replaces it with simply "aircraft" — covering all parts of chapter 88 goods and tariff headings 8802.30.00 and 8802.40.00 (aircraft and spacecraft).
→
✈ NICHE
Aviation industry & drone operators benefit. Cheaper to import aircraft parts legally. Probably won't affect your daily life — unless you own a plane 😅
PART VI – MISCELLANEOUS FEES & LEVIES
Section 54 · Miscellaneous Fees & Levies Act s.9
Import Declaration Fee — Scope Broadened
The words "import declaration fee, railway development levy and export levy" are replaced with simply "the fees and levies imposed under Part III". This makes the IDF exemption formula more general and future-proof — any new levy under Part III automatically falls under it.
→
📝 ADMIN
Legal housekeeping — makes the law flexible. Won't hit your pocket directly but shows government wants room to add new levies later without amending this section again.
